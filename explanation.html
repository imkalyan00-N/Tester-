Module 4: The Reality Check

STEP 01: The Illusion of Happiness
Module 4: Happiness and Prosperity – Current Scenario:
In the current scenario, we are generally trying to achieve happiness and prosperity by maximizing accumulation and consumption of physical facilities. This is an attempt to achieve happiness through pleasant sensory interactions. The physical facilities are not seen in terms of fulfilling bodily needs but as a means of maximizing happiness.

🏮 Ippudu janalu em chestunnaru? (The Illusion)

Present rojullo janalu 'Happiness & Prosperity' kavalante em chestunnaru? Pichipichiga dabbulu, vasthuvulu (Physical facilities) koodabettukuni (accumulation), viparithanga vaadeskuntunnaru (consumption).

Nijam enti ante: Car lu, AC lu, costly gadgets vaadite vache sensory pleasures (Kanti choopu, ruchi, touch) lalone santhosham undi anukuntunnaru. Aa vasthuvulni body avasarala kosam kaakunda, 'Veetithone naku santhosham vastundi' ane bhrama lo vaadutunnaru.

STEP 02: Unlimited Wants vs Limited Resources
This has resulted in wrong assessment of wants for physical facilities as being unlimited. But this pursuit is self-defeating. Neither can we hope to achieve continuous happiness through sensory interactions nor can we have prosperity, as it amounts to trying to fulfil unlimited wants through limited resources. This effort is engendering problems at all the levels. It is becoming anti-ecological and anti-people, and threatening the human survival itself. Some of the consequences of such a trend are summarized below:

🏮 Korikalu Unlimited.. Resources Limited!

Paina cheppina mind-set valla manushula korikalu (wants) Unlimited (Anantham) aypoyayi. Entha unna saripovatledu. Kani manam undedi limit unna resources unna prapanchamlo. "Unlimited korikalni.. Limited resources tho" theerchukolem.

Ila dabbu ventapadi parigettadam valla... idi manushulaki (anti-people), prakruthi ki (anti-ecological) chala danger ga maarindi. Asalu manishi manugadake (survival) pramadham ga maarindi. Deeni valla vache kastalu (consequences) kinda chuddam:

STEP 03: The 4 Levels of Disaster
1 At the level of the individual – Rising problems of depression, psychological disorders, suicides, stress, insecurity, psycho-somatic diseases, loneliness etc.
2 At the level of the family – Breaking of joint families, mistrust, conflict between older and younger generations, insecurity in relationships, divorce, dowry tortures, family feuds, wasteful expenditure in family functions etc.
3 At the level of the Society – Growing incidences of terrorism and naxalism, rising communalism, spreading casteism, racial and ethnic struggle, wars between nations, attempts of genocide, fear of nuclear and genetic warfare, etc.
4 At the level of nature – Global warming, water, air, soil, noise, etc. pollution, resource depletion of minerals and mineral oils, sizeable deforestations, loss of fertility of soil.

🏮 Dabbu Pichi valla vachina 4 Nastalu (Consequences)

Manishi dabbu, vasthuvula venta parigettadam valla 4 levels lo em jarugutundo chudu:

1. Individual (Nee level lo): Andariki Depression, stress, suicide thoughts, ontarithanam (loneliness), mental tension valla vache rogalu (psycho-somatic diseases) perigipoyayi.

2. Family (Kutumbam lo): Joint families viddipovadam, nammakam lekapovadam (mistrust), pelli lalo satha koti panikimalina karchulu (wasteful expenditure), divorces, dowry tortures perigayi.

3. Society (Samajam lo): Terrorism, Kulalu-Mathala madhya godavalu (communalism & casteism), desala madhya yuddhalu (wars), nuclear bombs bhayam perigindi.

4. Nature (Prakruthi lo): Global warming, Air/Water pollution, nelaboggu/petrol aypovadam, adavulu narakadam (deforestation) perigipoyayi.

STEP 04: The Urgent Need
It therefore, calls for an urgent need for human beings to correctly understand happiness and prosperity as well as the sustainable way to achieve these.

🏮 Ippudu Urgent ga em kavali?

Ee darunalu anni chusaka.. ippudu urgent ga manushulaki Nijamaina Happiness (Santhosham) mariyu Prosperity (Samrudhi) ante ento.. vaatini nature ni paadu cheyakunda (sustainable ga) ela achieve cheyalo theliyalsina avasaram undi.

STEP 05: SVDD vs SSDD (Exam Question)
Today we are not working according to this that why we can see that there are two kind of people in the world:
1. Those that do not have physical facilities/ wealth and feel unhappy and deprived.
i.e. SVDD: SadhanViheenDukhiDaridra – Materially Deficient, Unhappy and Deprived.
2. Those that have physical facilities/ wealth and feel unhappy and deprived. i.e.
SSDD: SadhanSampannDukhiDaridra – Materially Affluent, Unhappy and Deprived.

But these are states we don’t want to be in. We want to move from this category.
Presently, as we look around, we find most of the people in the above two categories called SVDD and SSDD.

🏮 SVDD vs SSDD (Prapancham lo 2 rakala janalu)

Sarayina avagahana (Right understanding) lekapovadam valla prapanchamlo janalu 2 categories ga vidipoyaru:

1. SVDD (Sadhan Viheen Dukhi Daridra): Veella daggara dabbu undadu, physical facilities undavu (Sadhan Viheen). Anduke veellu eppudu baadha paduthu, anathalaga (Unhappy and Deprived) feel avthuntaru.

2. SSDD (Sadhan Sampann Dukhi Daridra): Ee category valle ippudu ekkuva. Veella daggara masthu dabbulu, asthulu, facilities untayi (Sadhan Sampann / Materially affluent). Kani veellu kuda baadha paduthu eppudu edo thakkuva ayyindane feel avthuntaru (Unhappy and Deprived).

The Ultimate Truth: Manam ee rendu categories (SVDD leda SSDD) lo ekkada undakudadu. Kani bayataki velli chuste prapancham lo andaroo ee renditlo edo oka danilone irukkupoyaru. Ee mind-set nunchi bayataki raavali!
