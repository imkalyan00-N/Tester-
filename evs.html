Unit 1: Environmental Pollution

1. Pollution & Pollutant

  • Pollution: Derived from Latin word 'polluère' (to contaminate). Undesirable change in physical, chemical, or biological characteristics of air, water, and land.
  • Pollutant: Any substance which causes pollution is known as a pollutant.

2. Classification of Pollutants

Memory Trick: "DEN"

Pollutants ni 3 types ga vidadeeyochu. Just DEN gurthu pettuko:

  • D = Disposal (Bhoomi lo kalisipoye teeru)
  • E = Existence (Nature lo unna status)
  • N = Nature/Origin (Puttuka / Ekkadi nunchi vachai)

Based on Disposal (D)

  • Biodegradable: Degraded by natural decomposition. (Eg: Veg peels, paper).
  • Non-degradable: Slowly or not degraded. (Eg: Plastic, glass, metal).
🗣️ Explanation: Biodegradable ante matti lo kalisipoyevi (kura gayala thokkalu). Non-degradable ante entha kaalam aina matti lo kalavani vi (Plastic).

Based on Existence (E)

  • Quantitative: Present in nature, but dangerous when threshold is exceeded. (Eg: CO2).
  • Qualitative: Not present in nature, added by humans. (Eg: Pesticides, toxic chemicals).
🗣️ Explanation: Quantitative ante nature lo untai, kani limit daatithe danger (Like CO2). Qualitative ante asalu nature lo undavu, maname srushtincham (Like chemicals).

Based on Origin (N)

  • Primary: Released directly from source. (Eg: Ash, CO2, SOx).
  • Secondary: Formed by combination of primary pollutants in presence of light. (Eg: Smog, PAN).
🧠 Trick for Smog Equations:
S + F = S ➔ Smoke + Fog = Smog (1952 London)
S + P + Sun = PS ➔ Smog + PAN + Sunlight = Photochemical Smog (1956 LA)

Unit 2: Noise Pollution

Definition: Derived from Latin word 'Nausea' (feeling of sickness in stomach). Presence of unwanted or unpleasant sounds in the environment.

1. Effects of Noise Pollution

Memory Trick: "BP HIGH"

Noise valla vache rogalu gurtupettukovadaniki BP HIGH ani gurtupettuko:

  • B - Behavior changes / Blood Pressure peruguddi
  • P - Peptic Ulcers (Gastro problems)
  • H - Hearing loss (Chevudu)
  • I - Insomnia (Sleeplessness/Nidralemi)
  • G - Gastro-intestinal disorders
  • H - Heart beat peruguddi (Adrenaline)
🗣️ Explanation: Pedda soundlu vinnappudu bhayam vesi gunde gattiga kottukuntundi (Heartbeat/BP). Tension ki kadupulo acidlu vachi ulcers vasthai (Gastro). Rathrullu sound ki nidrapattadu (Insomnia). Naadi vyavastha debbathini chiraku vasthundi (Behavior).

2. Control Measures

Memory Trick: "S - P - R"

Noise ni 3 chotla apochu: Source ➔ Path ➔ Receiver

  • At Source (Puttina daggara): Use silencers in automobiles, use lubricants, regular servicing of vehicles.
  • At Path (Dhaarilo): Increase distance, increase plantation (trees absorb sound), industries away from residential areas.
  • At Receiver (Vinedi maname): Use cotton balls, ear plugs, ear muffs.

Unit 3: Water Pollution

  • Point Sources: Cause & place are easily identified. (Eg: Industrial effluents, oil wells).
  • Non-Point Sources: Cause & place are NOT easily identified. (Eg: Agricultural run-off, acid rains).

1. Diseases due to Water Pollution (Very Imp)

Disease Matching Tricks

👉 M - M (Minamata = Mercury)
👉 I - C (Itai Itai = Cadmium)
👉 B - B - N (Blue Baby = Nitrates)
👉 F - F (Fluorosis = Fluorine)
  • Minamata Disease: Caused by Mercury (Hg). (Japan 1953, Chisso corp. Vision loss, paralysis).
  • Itai-Itai: Caused by Cadmium (Cd). (Japan 1953. Bones bending, kidney damage).
  • Blue Baby Syndrome: Presence of Nitrates.
  • Fluorosis: Excess Fluorine. (Mottling/yellowing of teeth, bending of bones).
  • Bacterial: Cholera (Vibrio cholerae), Typhoid (Salmonella typhi), Amoebiasis.
  • Red Tide Effect: Death of fishes (more at a time).

2. Three Main Concepts (DO, BOD, COD)

🧠 Logic Trick: Pollution perigithe ➔ DO thagguthundi ⬇️, BOD & COD peruguthai ⬆️.
  • DO (Dissolved Oxygen): Amount of oxygen dissolved in water at particular temp and pressure.
  • BOD (Biological Oxygen Demand): Amount of oxygen required for aerobic decomposition of organic matter by microbes over 5 days at 20°C.
  • COD (Chemical Oxygen Demand): Measures all oxygen consuming pollutant matter.
🗣️ Explanation: Neetilo chettha (pollution) padithe, bacteria aa chettani thinesi kullabeduthundi. Ala cheyadaniki bacteria ki oxygen kavali, daanne BOD antaru. Bacteria oxygen vadesukuntundi kabatti, neetilo unna normal oxygen (DO) thaggipothundi!

3. Bioaccumulation & Eutrophication

  • Eutrophication: Enrichment of water bodies with nutrients (Nitrates/Phosphates). Leads to phytoplankton blooms, reduces O2 & sunlight, fishes die.
  • Bioaccumulation: Accumulation of toxic substances from abiotic to biotic component (living organisms).
  • Biomagnification: Increase of toxic concentration from one tropic level to another in food chain. (Effects: Life span reduced, reproductive failure).
🗣️ Explanation for Biomagnification: Neetilo chinna visham unna, danni thunna chinna chepalo visham cherthundi. Alaanti 10 chinna chepalani oka pedda chepa thintundi, appudu aa visham power (concentration) inka peruguthundi. Daanne Biomagnification antaru.

Control Measures (Important Bits):

  • Pseudomonas putida: Bacteria used to remove oil slick (samudram lo oil leaks clean cheyadaniki).
  • Water hyacinth: Plant used to filter out heavy metals (Hg, Cd, Pb).

Unit 4: Air Pollution

1. Effects on Plants

Memory Trick: "C - N - E"

Mokkalapai prabhavam gurtupettukovadaniki CNE trick:

  • Chlorosis = Colour loss (Sulphur valla)
  • Necrosis = Nerves (cells) die (Nitrogen valla)
  • Epinasty = Edges (tips) curl backwards
  • SOx -> Chlorosis: Loss of chlorophyll.
  • NOx -> Necrosis: Cellular disintegration.
  • NOx & Ethylene -> Epinasty: Curling of leaf tips backwards.

2. Effects on Humans (Match the following list)

  • CO (Carbon Monoxide): Forms Carboxyhaemoglobin (COHb), reduces O2 carrying capacity.
  • Mine Dust (SPM): Causes Black Lung Disease.
  • Asbestos fibers: Asbestosis. Silicon: Silicosis.
  • Lead (Pb): Damage to RBC cells & Brain.
  • Pollen grains: Sneezing effect, asthma, allergy.
  • Microbial spores: T.B (Tuberculosis).
🗣️ Explanation for CO: Carbon Monoxide chala danger. Manam peelchinappudu mana blood loni Haemoglobin oxygen ni vadilesi, ee CO tho kalisi COHb ga maripothundi. Oxygen andaka manishi sastadu.

3. Effects on Materials & Control

🧠 Equation Trick: Marble + Acid = Stone Leprosy (Taj Mahal fading).
CaCO3 + H2SO4 ➔ CaSO4 + H2O + CO2

Control Equipments to remember: Use CNG, Bio-fuels (Ethanol). Factories lo Scrubbers, Cyclones, Electrostatic Precipitators (ESPs) vadali.

Unit 5: Solid Waste Management

1. Components of MSW (Muncipal Solid Waste)

Memory Trick: "GRATCS"

Chetta rakaalu: Garbage, Rubbish, Ash, Trash, C&D, Special waste.

  • Garbage: Biodegradable (Eg: Kitchen waste).
  • Rubbish: Non-biodegradable. Combustible (plastic/rubber) & Non-combustible (glass/rock).
  • Trash: Bulky waste (Eg: old sofas, fridges).
  • C&D Waste: Construction & Demolition (cement, bricks).

2. Treatment Methods (Very Important)

Memory Trick: "Incineration vs Pyrolysis"

  • INcineration = IN presence of Oxygen.
  • PYrolysis = Bypassing Oxygen (Absence of O2).
  • Incineration: Burning of waste in presence of Oxygen.
  • Pyrolysis: Burning of waste in absence of Oxygen. (Used for burning rubber).
  • Shredding: Cutting & tearing waste (Rotatory shear shredder).
  • Pulverizing: Crushing & grinding waste in Hammer mills.
  • Baling/Compaction: Conversion of waste into rectangular blocks in presses.
  • Composting: Organic waste to compost by aerobic decomposition.
  • Vermi-composting: Composting using earthworms.
  • Co-composting: Organic waste + Sewage sludge.
🗣️ Explanation: Pulverizing ante sutthi tho pindi ga kottadam (Hammer mills). Baling ante pedda chettani chinna block laaga isthri petti nokkadam, space thakkuva teeskovadaniki. Vermi-composting lo earthworms (vaanapaamulu) matti ni thinesi eruvu ga marchuthai.

Unit 6: Global Issues

Mega Trick for Protocols

Rendu protocols unnai. Confuse avvakunda ila gurtupettuko:

  • K - G ➔ Kyoto = Greenhouse (Global Warming)
  • M - O ➔ Montreal = Ozone Layer

1. Global Warming & Kyoto

  • Green House Gases (GHG): CO2, CH4, CFCs, NOx, O3, Water vapour.
  • Impacts: Melting of polar ice caps, sea level rise, vector borne diseases (malaria, dengue).
  • Kyoto Protocol (1997): Held in Japan to decrease GHG.
🗣️ Explanation: Ee gases anni bhoomi paina oka glass laaga form ayyi, lopala unna vedi (heat) ni bayatiki vellaniyyavu. Anduke bhoomi vedekkuthundi (Global Warming).

2. Ozone Depletion & Montreal

  • Ozone Depleting Substances (ODS): CFCs, NOx.
  • Measurement: Thickness measured in Dobson Unit (D.U). First hole in 1985 (Antarctica).
  • Effects: Skin cancer, Cataracts, Albinism, gene mutations.
  • Montreal Protocol (1987): Held in Canada to reduce ODS. (World Ozone Day = Sep 16).
🗣️ Explanation: AC lu, Fridges nunchi vache CFC gas velli Ozone pora ki bokka peduthundi. Daanivalla Surya kiranalalo unde UV rays direct ga mana meeda padi skin cancer vasthundi.

3. Sustainable Development

🧠 Trick for Pillars: E-E-S-I (Environmental, Economical, Social, Institutional).
  • Definition: Development that meets the needs of present generation without compromising the ability of future generations.
  • Coined by G.H. Bruntland in 1992 UNCED (Earth Summit, Rio de Janeiro).
  • Obstacles (Pitfalls): Population explosion, lack of funds, non-ecofriendly technology.

Unit 7: Health & Population

1. HIV / AIDS

Memory Tricks

  • Tests: E - W (ELISA, Western Blot)
  • Drugs: Z - Drugs (Zidovudine)
  • AIDS: Acquired Immuno Deficiency Syndrome. (First case USA 1981, India Chennai 1986).
  • Transmission: Blood transfusion, common needles/razors, mother to baby, unprotected sex.
  • Does NOT spread: Mosquito bites, sharing meals/toilets, hugging.
  • Symptoms: Weight loss, swollen lymph nodes, immunity reduced.
  • Treatment: Anti-viral therapy, Zidovudine drugs.
  • World AIDS Day: Dec 1st.

2. Population Growth

Memory Tricks for Control

  • Men ➔ Vasectomy
  • Women ➔ Tubectomy
  • Derived from Latin word 'populus' (people).
  • Reasons: Child marriages, increased natality (birth rate), decreased mortality (death rate), preference for sons.
  • Terms: Doubling time (time to double pop). Infant Mortality Rate (babies dying per 1000).
  • Effects: Poverty, slums, deforestation, food scarcity.
  • Control: Education, prevent child marriages, family planning incentives (Vasectomy, Tubectomy).
  • World Population Day: July 11th.
