Hierarchical Clustering (Agglomerative Method) – Step-by-Step Process

Step 1: Initialization

Consider 8 data points:

x1, x2, x3, x4, x5, x6, x7, x8

Initially, each data point is treated as an individual cluster.

C1 = {x1}, {x2}, {x3}, {x4}, {x5}, {x6}, {x7}, {x8}

Step 2: Find the closest pairs and merge

Based on minimum distance:

  • Merge x2 and x3 → {x2, x3} (distance ≈ 2.5)
  • Merge x4 and x5 → {x4, x5} (distance ≈ 3.5)
  • Merge x6 and x7 → {x6, x7} (distance ≈ 1.25)

C2 = {x1}, {x2, x3}, {x4, x5}, {x6, x7}, {x8}

Step 3: Merge next closest cluster

  • Merge x1 with {x2, x3}

C3 = {x1, x2, x3}, {x4, x5}, {x6, x7}, {x8}

Step 4: Next merging

  • Merge x8 with {x4, x5}

C4 = {x1, x2, x3}, {x4, x5, x8}, {x6, x7}

Step 5: Merge larger clusters

  • Merge {x6, x7} with {x4, x5, x8}

C5 = {x6, x7, x8, x4, x5}, {x1, x2, x3}

Step 6: Final merge

  • Merge all clusters

C6 = {x1, x2, x3, x4, x5, x6, x7, x8}

Final Clusters

C4 = {x1, x2, x3}

C5 = {x4, x5, x8}

C6 = {x6, x7, x8, x4, x5}

C7 = {x1, x2, x3, x4, x5, x6, x7, x8}
