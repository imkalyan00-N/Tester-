Hierarchical Clustering (Divisive Method) – Step-by-Step Process

Step 1: Initialization

Consider 8 data points:

x1, x2, x3, x4, x5, x6, x7, x8

Initially, all data points are in a single cluster.

C1 = {x1, x2, x3, x4, x5, x6, x7, x8}

Step 2: First Split

Split the main cluster into two clusters:

  • {x1, x2, x3, x8}
  • {x4, x6, x5, x7}

C2 = {x1, x2, x3, x8}, {x4, x6, x5, x7}

Step 3: Further Split

Split each cluster into smaller clusters:

  • {x1, x2, x3, x8} → {x1, x3} and {x2, x8}
  • {x4, x6, x5, x7} → {x6, x7} and {x4, x5}

C3 = {x1, x3}, {x2, x8}, {x6, x7}, {x4, x5}

Step 4: Final Split

Split all clusters into individual elements:

  • {x1, x3} → {x1}, {x3}
  • {x2, x8} → {x2}, {x8}
  • {x6, x7} → {x6}, {x7}
  • {x4, x5} → {x4}, {x5}

C4 = {x1}, {x2}, {x3}, {x4}, {x5}, {x6}, {x7}, {x8}
